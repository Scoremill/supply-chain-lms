# Module 8: Final Mechanicals & Exterior Completion
## Introduction Script — Narrator: J.P.

---

[look directly at camera, focused]

Welcome back. I'm J.P., and this is Module Eight — Final Mechanicals and Exterior Completion.

[pause]

We're in the home stretch. The interior is painted, trimmed, and floored. But we're not done. This module covers where all those mechanical systems from Module Five come to life — and where the exterior gets finished. During rough-in, the electrician pulled wire, the plumber ran pipe, the HVAC contractor installed ductwork. All inspected and approved — but none of it functional yet. No lights, no water, no air. Final mechanicals is where we connect, energize, test, and commission every system.

[gesture outward]

First — electrical final. Outlets, switches, dimmers, GFCI receptacles get installed. Fixtures hung. Panel labeled and energized. Every circuit tested, every device verified, every GFCI and AFCI tripped. Smoke detectors, CO detectors, and specialty systems like low-voltage wiring all go in now.

Second — plumbing finals. Every toilet, faucet, showerhead, sink, disposal, and water heater connection. Testing for leaks at every connection, verifying pressure, confirming hot and cold are correct, testing drainage. Appliance connections too — dishwasher, ice maker, washing machine. Every connection is a potential leak point.

[look at camera]

Third — HVAC commissioning. More than flipping a switch. Verifying refrigerant charge, checking airflow at every register, balancing the system room by room, testing thermostat sequences, confirming combustion appliances draft properly. Improper commissioning is one of the most common homeowner complaints after move-in.

[step slightly]

Now outside. Fourth — concrete flatwork. Driveways, sidewalks, patios, garage floors, porches. Subgrade prep, forming, reinforcement, finishing, and curing. It's visible every day, so finish quality matters.

Fifth — landscaping. Finish grading for proper drainage away from the foundation, topsoil, seeding or sod, planting beds, mulch. Proper grading is a code requirement that protects against water intrusion.

[serious]

Sixth — final inspection. The city verifies all mechanical, electrical, and plumbing installations meet code. Fixture connections, device installations, system operation, smoke and CO placement, exterior grading. One of the last inspections before the Certificate of Occupancy.

[conversational]

The CM is running a tight ship — trades inside and outside, all finishing on deadline. Sequencing mechanical work, coordinating concrete pours around weather, managing landscape, preparing for inspection.

[slight nod]

Let's bring it all together.
