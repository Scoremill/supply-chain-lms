# Module 5: Mechanical, Electrical, and Plumbing Rough-In (MEPs)
## Introduction Script — Narrator: J.P.

---

[look directly at camera, focused]

Welcome back. I'm J.P., and this is Module Five — Mechanical, Electrical, and Plumbing Rough-In. MEPs for short — one of the most complex stages of the entire build.

[pause]

Here's the reality. Up to now, we've dealt with one or two trades at a time. In MEPs, three licensed trades — HVAC, plumbing, electrical — all work inside the same walls, floors, and ceilings simultaneously. That's a recipe for conflict if not managed properly.

[gesture]

This is where the construction manager earns their paycheck. Full stop.

[educational tone]

First — HVAC. Conditioned air to every room. Load calculations, furnace or air handler install, ductwork, combustion air and venting, exhaust in bathrooms and kitchens. Oversized is just as problematic as undersized. Leaky ducts waste energy and hurt comfort.

Second — plumbing. Supply and drain lines through walls and floors. Hot and cold to every fixture, plus the drain-waste-vent system. Venting is one of the most misunderstood aspects — we'll break down why it matters. Rough-in dimensions must be precise — off by half an inch and the fixture won't fit at final install.

[look at camera]

Third — electrical. Wire to every outlet, switch, light, and dedicated circuit. Load calculations, panel install, branch circuit design, wire sizing, and box placement. AFCI and GFCI requirements have expanded in recent code cycles — you need to know where they're required.

[more serious]

After all three trades finish and before insulation or drywall, the rough-in inspection happens. One of the most thorough — pipe sizing, duct sealing, wire gauge, box fill, nail plates, fire stopping. We'll cover inspection points and common violations. A failed rough-in means every trade comes back for corrections — easily a week lost.

[gesture to self]

The CM's responsibilities are intense. Trade scheduling — who goes first, who follows. Coordination meetings to resolve conflicts before they become field problems. Quality walkthroughs before calling inspection.

[conversational]

The best CMs treat MEPs like chess — thinking three moves ahead. HVAC trunk line before the drain stack in the same cavity. Electrician needs wall access before ductwork covers it. That foresight keeps projects on schedule.

[slight nod]

Let's get into the rough-in.
