# Module 10: Final Walkthrough & Handover
## Introduction Script — Narrator: J.P.

---

[look directly at camera, composed and reflective]

Welcome back. I'm J.P., and this is Module Ten — Final Walkthrough and Handover. The last module in "How to Build a Home."

[pause]

We started on a raw piece of dirt talking about surveys and site prep. Now we're inside a finished home — systems commissioned, finishes complete, cleaning done. But building the house is only part of the job. Handing it over properly and completely — that's what separates a good builder from a great one.

[deliberate]

This module covers everything between "the house is built" and "the homeowner has the keys." Inspections, legal documentation, homeowner education, financial reconciliation, and a formal handover process.

[gesture outward]

First — the CM's pre-final inspection. Before any inspector or homeowner enters, you walk it yourself. Room by room, system by system. If there's a deficiency, you find it now.

Second — municipal final inspection. The city determines whether the home meets code and is safe for occupancy. We'll cover what they evaluate, common deficiencies, and how to prepare.

[look at camera]

Third — the Certificate of Occupancy. The legal document authorizing occupancy. Without it, the homeowner can't move in and utilities often won't activate. We'll cover documentation required and conditions that sometimes attach.

Fourth — homeowner orientation. A structured session walking through every system — safety, HVAC, plumbing shutoffs, electrical panel, appliances. All demonstrated so the homeowner can safely operate their home from day one.

[serious]

Fifth — closeout documentation and financials. Lien releases from every trade. As-builts. Warranty docs. O&M manuals. Final payment processing, change order reconciliation, budget closeout.

Sixth — formal handover. Key transfer, punch list verification, warranty procedures, maintenance transition. The relationship doesn't end at key transfer — there's a warranty to manage and a reputation to protect.

[warmth]

Ten modules. Dirt to doorknob. Every trade, every inspection, every detail has led to this — handing a family the keys to their new home.

I've been doing this a long time, and there's nothing like that moment. The homeowner walks in, looks around, and you see it on their face — this is home.

[closing with conviction]

Thank you for taking this course. Build with integrity. Manage with discipline. Finish with pride.

[deliver with purpose]
