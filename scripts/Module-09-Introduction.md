# Module 9: Final Finishes & Cleaning
## Introduction Script — Narrator: J.P.

---

[look directly at camera, confident and relaxed]

Welcome back. I'm J.P., and this is Module Nine — Final Finishes and Cleaning.

[pause]

We are close. Systems are in, finishes installed, exterior complete. But before a homeowner walks through that front door, there's a critical phase that separates a construction site from a finished home — the final details that elevate the project from "built" to "move-in ready."

[more serious]

This is the phase builders rush through. They're tired, the project's been going months, the homeowner's anxious. That's exactly when mistakes happen. Appliances installed without testing. Hardware slapped on without alignment. Touch-up skipped. Cleaning half-hearted. The result? A punch list a mile long and a disappointed homeowner.

[lean in]

That's not how we do it. This module teaches you to close out with the same discipline you brought to the foundation.

[gesture outward]

Five major areas, each one matters.

First — appliance installation. Every major appliance delivered, installed, connected, tested. Water lines, gas hookups, electrical, leveling, clearances. Getting a refrigerator through a finished house without damage requires planning. "Plugging it in" isn't the same as "verifying it works."

Second — architectural hardware. Locksets, hinges, stops, towel bars, knobs and pulls. Every door should open, close, latch, and lock smoothly — adjust now, not after move-in. Key management at turnover must be organized.

[look at camera]

Third — paint touch-up. After all trades, there's damage — scuffs, nicks, scratches. Identify areas needing attention, verify color and sheen matches, know when a full re-coat is required. The goal: you can't tell where touch-up happened.

Fourth — construction cleaning. Not just sweeping. Rough cleaning, final cleaning, detail cleaning. Surface-specific protocols, label removal, window cleaning. Plus HVAC cleaning and new filters — ductwork has collected dust for months.

[straighten up]

Fifth — the CM's final responsibilities. Punch list inspection, categorization by severity, substantial completion certification, C of O prep, owner training, documentation, warranty responsibilities. Room by room, system by system.

[gesture to self]

The CM is the last line of defense. Before the homeowner sees the house, you've walked every room, tested every fixture, opened every door, flushed every toilet. That's the standard.

[slight nod]

Let's finish strong.
