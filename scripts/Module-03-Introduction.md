# Module 3: Framing
## Introduction Script — Narrator: J.P.

---

[look directly at camera, energized]

Welcome back. I'm J.P., and this is Module Three — Framing.

[slight smile]

We've gone from raw dirt to a finished foundation. Now it's time to go vertical. Framing is one of the most exciting phases — walls go up, the roof takes shape, and you can walk through the space for the first time. But don't let the excitement fool you. Framing is technically demanding. Every wall, header, joist, and rafter has to be built to code. If your framing is off, every trade that follows has problems. Plumbers can't rough in correctly, cabinets won't sit flat, drywall won't hang straight.

[gesture outward]

We're walking through the entire framing process from the ground up.

First — floor system construction. Sill plate anchored to the foundation, joists set, subfloor goes down. Anchor bolt spacing, joist sizing, bridging, fastener patterns — all of it matters. The floor system needs to be level, square, and solid.

Then wall assembly — the heart of framing. Studs, plates, headers, king studs, jack studs — we'll cover why each exists. Quality standards: plumb, level, straight, properly fastened. Header sizing for openings is critical — an undersized header is a structural deficiency the inspector will catch.

[look at camera]

Next, roof and ceiling framing. Stick-framed rafters or engineered trusses — proper bearing, connections, and sheathing. Ridge boards, collar ties, ceiling joists, and how the roof transfers load through the walls to the foundation. Improper nailing patterns on sheathing can compromise the entire system.

We'll also cover window and door installation. Rough opening sizing, flashing details, and weather barrier integration. These are penetrations in your envelope — framed incorrectly, you're inviting water intrusion.

[straighten up]

The framing inspection is one of the most comprehensive on the project. Structural connections, fastener schedules, fire blocking, header sizes, bracing — the inspector checks it all. We'll cover what they look for so you can self-inspect before calling.

[gesture to self]

The construction manager coordinates everything — material deliveries, verifying work against plans, resolving field conflicts, preparing for inspection. The CM's detail here determines how smoothly the rest flows.

[look at camera with intent]

This is where the house takes shape. Let's frame it right.
