# Module 1: Site Preparation & Pre-Construction Operations
## Introduction Script — Narrator: J.P.

---

[look directly at camera, confident and welcoming]

Hey there — I'm J.P., and welcome to "How to Build a Home," brought to you by Strategem. Over the next ten modules, we're walking through the entire process of building a residential home — dirt to doorknob. I'm not going to sugarcoat it — this is complex. But that's exactly why you're here.

[slight pause, shift tone]

With Strategem, you'll learn how to Build Faster, Spend Smarter, and Deliver More.

[lean in slightly]

Before we pour concrete or swing a hammer, there's a critical phase that separates professionals from amateurs — site preparation and pre-construction. This is Module One, and if you get this wrong, everything after is an uphill battle.

[gesture for emphasis]

I've seen projects go sideways because someone skipped a step here — didn't verify the survey, didn't confirm setbacks, didn't coordinate site work. Those mistakes cost real money, and sometimes the whole project.

[shift to educational tone]

We're covering four key areas in this module.

First — the surveyor. This professional establishes the legal and physical boundaries of your project. Property lines, easements, setbacks, elevation benchmarks. Without accurate survey data, you're guessing where to put the house. And guessing doesn't pass inspection.

Second — site preparation contractor operations. This crew transforms raw land into a buildable lot. Clearing, grading, erosion control, access roads, utility rough-ins — all in the right sequence. We'll cover what proper grading looks like, why drainage matters from day one, and why erosion control is a legal requirement, not just best practice.

[look directly at camera]

Third — the municipal inspector. Inspections can feel like a headache, but the inspector is your ally if you understand what they need. We'll walk through code requirements, what triggers an inspection, and how to be ready before they show up. Nothing slows a project down like a failed inspection.

And fourth — the construction manager's role in pre-construction. The CM orchestrates everything: scheduling, coordination, quality control, documentation, budget tracking. They make sure every trade, inspector, and delivery is in the right place at the right time.

[more conversational]

The projects that run smooth are the ones where the CM was dialed in from the beginning. Not from framing — from site prep. That's where the tone gets set.

[look at camera with intent]

Pay attention to how these roles connect. The surveyor feeds information to the site prep crew. Site prep gets verified by the inspector. And the construction manager ties it all together.

[slight smile]

This is where it starts. Let's get into it.
